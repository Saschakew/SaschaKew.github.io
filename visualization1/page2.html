Using the simplifications from the previous page, the reduced form shocks $u_t$ and consequently the macroeconomnic variables $y_t$ can be thought of a with angle $\phi_0$ of the structural form shocks $\epsilon_t$: $$ \begin{bmatrix} u_{1,t} \\ u_{2,t} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\phi_0) & -\sin(\phi_0) \\ \sin(\phi_0) & \cos(\phi_0) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \epsilon_{1,t} \\ \epsilon_{2,t} \end{bmatrix} $$

In the simulation below, the structural form shocks are independently drawn from a uniform distribution.i
Move the $\phi_0$ slider above to see how $B(\phi_0)$ leads to different rotations $u_t$ of $\epsilon_t$.
