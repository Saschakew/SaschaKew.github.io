So how can we verify whether we picked $\phi=\phi_0$ and whether our inovations are equal to the structural shocks?

While we cannot observe the structural shocks $\epsilon_t$, we do know something about their stochastic properties: The structural shocks $\epsilon_t$ are independent, meaning a given shock $\epsilon_{1,t}$ contains no information on the shock $\epsilon_{2,t}$ and vice versa. The same stochastic property should apply to the innovations $e_t(B(\phi))$, meaning a given innovation $e_{1,t}$ should contain no information on the innovation $e_{2,t}$ and vice versa.

We can use the stochastic property of independent shocks to verify whether we have chosen $ \phi = \phi_0 $ and whether our innovations are equal to the structural shocks. We can do this in two steps:

  1. Propose a rotation angle $ \phi $ and calculate the corresponding innovations $ e_t(B(\phi)) $.
  2. Measure the dependency of the innovations and rule out rotation angles $ \phi $ that lead to dependent innovations.

The dependency of the innovations $ e_t(B(\phi)) $ can be measured using moment conditions. Specifically, independent shocks with mean zero and unit variance will satistfy the following covariance-, coskewness-, and cokurtosis- conditions $$ \begin{matrix} \text{Covariance condition} &E[\varepsilon_{1,t}\varepsilon_{2,t}] = 0 &\implies& E[e_{1,t}(B)e_{2,t}(B)] \overset{!}{=} 0 \\ \text{Coskewness condition} &E[\varepsilon_{1,t}^2\varepsilon_{2,t}] = 0 & \implies & E[e_{1,t}(B)^2e_{2,t}(B)] \overset{!}{=} 0 \\ \text{Coskewness condition} &E[\varepsilon_{1,t}\varepsilon_{2,t}^2]=0 & \implies & E[e_{1,t}(B)e_{2,t}(B)^2] \overset{!}{=} 0 \\ \text{Cokurtosis condition} &E[\varepsilon_{1,t}^3\varepsilon_{2,t}] = 0 &\implies& E[e_{1,t}(B)^3 e_{2,t}(B)] \overset{!}{=} 0 \\ \text{Cokurtosis condition} &E[\varepsilon_{1,t}\varepsilon_{2,t}^3] = 0 &\implies& E[e_{1,t}(B) e_{2,t}(B)^3] \overset{!}{=} 0 \\ \text{Cokurtosis condition} &E[\varepsilon_{1,t}^2\varepsilon_{2,t}^2] = 1 &\implies& E[e_{1,t}(B)^2 e_{2,t}(B)^2] \overset{!}{=} 1 \end{matrix} $$
